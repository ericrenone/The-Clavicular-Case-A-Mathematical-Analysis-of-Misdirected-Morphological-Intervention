# The Clavicular Case: A Mathematical Analysis of Misdirected Morphological Intervention
## Applying Col/Ker Theory and Polyscale Persistence to Extreme Looksmaxxing

---

## Executive Summary

Braden Eric Peters (online alias: Clavicular) is implementing a systematic program of facial modification through:
- Surgical intervention (rhinoplasty, otoplasty, wisdom teeth extraction)
- Pharmacological intervention (testosterone, crystal meth, steroids, Accutane, dutasteride, minoxidil, anavar, retatrutide)
- Mechanical trauma (bonesmashing with a hammer)
- Planned additional surgery (bimaxillary osteotomy, limb-lengthening)

**Core claim:** His program operates under a fundamental misunderstanding of what facial morphology determines and what morphology can change. Specifically, he is operating under the col/ker error: treating observable facial morphology (col(F)) as if modifying it will change unobservable character/trustworthiness/social value (ker(F)), when the mathematics show this is impossible at the individual level.

**Prediction:** Even if Clavicular successfully modifies his facial morphology (achieves col(F) matching his target), this will not produce the social/relational outcomes he seeks, because those outcomes are not determined by morphological changes alone. The polyscale persistence framework predicts that modifications at the biological timescale will be undermined by persistence at the institutional, biographical, and developmental timescales.

## Part One: Mathematical Analysis Using Col/Ker Framework

### The Transformation Function He Is Attempting to Modify

Recall the transformation function F: X → Y where:
- X = unobservable causal space (7-dimensional minimum)
- Y = observable facial morphology (10-dimensional)
- F = transformation mapping X to Y

From the previous analysis:

**Coefficient values in F:**
- X₁ (climate-ancestry): 82% of nasal width, 40-50% of orbital size, ~0% of expression lines
- X₂ (developmental): 12% of nasal width, 25% of orbital size, ~5% of expression lines
- X₃ (chronological aging): 0% of nasal width, 18% of orbital diameter, 15% of expression line depth
- X₄ (chronic stress/weathering): 2-3% of nasal width, 12% of orbital characteristics, 25-30% of expression lines
- X₅ (occupational emotional labor): 0% of nasal width, 0% of orbital characteristics, 45-50% of expression lines
- X₆ (character/personality): <1% of all observable morphology
- X₇ (random variation): 2-5%

### Clavicular's Intervention Strategy Analyzed

**Rhinoplasty (modifying nasal width and projection):**

The observable nasal morphology Y_nasal is determined by:

Y_nasal = 0.82·X₁ + 0.12·X₂ + 0.00·X₃ + 0.01·X₄ + 0.00·X₅ + 0.00·X₆ + 0.05·X₇

Clavicular can modify Y_nasal through surgical intervention. This changes the right-hand side of the equation by reducing the 0.00·X₆ term (character is not affecting nasal morphology) and instead making the nose match a target morphology Y_target.

**The critical problem:** Changing Y_nasal does not change X₆ (character/capability/trustworthiness). The nose was never determined by character. Changing the nose does not make character change.

Clavicular appears to believe: rhinoplasty → new face → improved character perception → improved social outcomes

But the mathematics show:
- Rhinoplasty → changes Y_nasal
- Changes in Y_nasal → may change observer perception (through amygdala-driven inference)
- Changed observer perception → changes treatment of Clavicular
- Changed treatment → may change actual behavior and development going forward (X₅, X₃ future trajectory)

**But:** The change in treatment is not automatic or guaranteed. It depends on whether:
1. Observers' inferences actually change (not guaranteed; may misattribute to other factors)
2. The changed treatment accumulates across enough interactions to affect behavior
3. The behavioral changes are in the direction Clavicular expects
4. Institutional systems codify the new treatment (rather than reverting to old patterns)

**The underdetermined outcome:** Even with successful rhinoplasty, Clavicular's social outcomes remain underdetermined because changing one component of col(F) does not reliably change observer behavior, which does not reliably change treatment, which does not reliably change actual capability.

### Bonesmashing: The Category Error Made Explicit

Bonesmashing is the mechanical trauma of striking the face with a hammer to induce micro-fractures in bone, with the goal of stimulating bone regrowth in a "sharper" configuration.

**The underlying theory:** Mechanical loading stimulates osteoblast activity. If you induce fractures, the bone remodeling process will create stronger, more prominent bone structure.

**The mathematical problem:** Bonesmashing is attempting to modify X₁ (climate-ancestral morphology), which is genetically determined and fixed at birth.

The transformation function shows:

Y_nasal = 0.82·X₁ + ...

X₁ is determined by:
- Parental alleles for bone density, growth factors
- Selection pressure from ancestral climate (already completed over 1000+ generations)
- Population mean genetic background

**Bonesmashing cannot change X₁ because:**
- X₁ is determined by inherited alleles, not by post-birth mechanical loading
- Selection pressure operates over population lifespans, not individual lifetimes
- The bone regrowth from mechanical trauma follows genetic instructions, not arbitrary new directions

What bonesmashing CAN do:
- Induce localized inflammation
- Trigger bone remodeling
- Possibly create slight morphological changes

But these changes are constrained by the genetic program that determines bone growth. You cannot force bone to grow in directions inconsistent with the genetic instructions.

**The category error:** Clavicular is treating his inherited genetic morphology (X₁) as if it can be modified through mechanical intervention, when X₁ is determined entirely by inheritance and ancestral selection pressure. Bonesmashing is attempting to solve a genetic problem through mechanical means.

**Empirical validation of the error:** If bonesmashing worked as Clavicular claims, we would see:
- Bone density increases specifically in the directions of hammer strikes
- Bone shape changes to match the trauma pattern
- These changes would be heritable (because they represent "improved" gene expressions)

None of this is observed. Bonesmashing produces:
- Local inflammation
- Possible slight bone thickening in the impact zone
- Pain and bruising
- Risk of permanent injury (nerve damage, eye damage, brain injury from repeated trauma)

The bone does not grow in new directions. It follows its genetic growth program.

### Substance Interventions: Which Variables Can Be Modified

**Testosterone (self-injected as teenager):**

Testosterone affects:
- Bone density (increases X₁-compatible growth)
- Muscle mass and tone
- Sebaceous gland activity (affects skin texture, part of X₄ weathering signal)

Effect on Y (observable morphology): Moderate (increases jaw prominence, increases muscle definition, affects skin texture)

**Problem:** Testosterone primarily enhances the genetic growth program (X₁), it does not redirect it. A person with genetically smaller mandible will have a somewhat larger mandible with exogenous testosterone, but will not achieve a mandible similar to someone with different genetic background.

**Crystal meth (to hollow cheeks and curb appetite):**

Mechanism: Methamphetamine causes:
- Suppression of appetite → caloric deficit → fat loss
- Increased sympathetic nervous system activity → increased metabolic rate
- Vasoconstriction → reduced blood flow to skin → skin damage
- Teeth grinding and poor dental care → tooth loss
- Systemic dehydration

Effect on Y: Rapid fat redistribution, skin damage, dental damage

This directly modifies soft tissue distribution (part of Y₃, Y₄ in the transformation function). But the effects are:
1. **Temporary:** Stop meth use, appetite returns, fat redistributes back
2. **Costly:** Severe health consequences (neurotoxicity, dental destruction, skin damage)
3. **Uncontrolled:** You cannot selectively reduce face fat while preserving health; the drug affects whole-body metabolism

**The polyscale problem:** Clavicular is using a substance with severe consequences at the chronological aging timescale (accelerated health decline, potential neurotoxicity) to produce temporary effects at the biographical timescale (current fat distribution). The long-term health cost vastly exceeds the morphological benefit.

**Anavar, minoxidil, Accutane, dutasteride, retatrutide:**

Each of these is a pharmacological intervention targeting a specific morphological variable:
- Anavar: Increases muscle mass and definition
- Minoxidil: Increases hair density
- Accutane: Reduces sebaceous gland activity (reduces acne/oiliness)
- Dutasteride: Blocks DHT to reduce facial hair and hair loss
- Retatrutide: Experimental GLP-1 agonist for rapid fat loss

**The mathematical problem:** Each intervention modifies one component of Y (a specific morphological feature), but none modifies X₆ (character). Clavicular is treating observable morphology as if changing it will change unobservable character.

Additionally, many of these substances have complex side effects that affect other timescales:
- Accutane is highly teratogenic
- Dutasteride affects sexual function
- Retatrutide (experimental) has unknown long-term effects
- Combined use creates unpredictable interactions

### The Fundamental Underdetermination

Even assuming Clavicular successfully achieves his target facial morphology (modifies col(F) to match his ideal), the transformation function shows:

**Outcome Y = F(X₁, X₂, X₃, X₄, X₅, X₆, X₇)**

He has modified:
- Y_1 through surgical modification of nasal structure
- Y_6, Y_7 through chronic stress effects and substance use affecting aging
- Y_10 through fat distribution changes from meth use

**He cannot modify:**
- X₁ (climate-ancestry) — this is determined at birth by inherited alleles
- X₆ (character/personality/trustworthiness) — this is not determined by facial morphology at all (coefficient <1%)

**The observers' inference problem:** When observers see the modified col(F), they will infer character using the amygdala-driven system:

Inference(character | modified col(F)) = ?

This inference depends on:
1. Whether observers interpret the morphological changes as "improvement"
2. Whether observers attribute the changes to underlying character (e.g., "disciplined," "trustworthy enough to get surgery") or to superficial modification (e.g., "vain," "unstable")
3. Whether the institutional context reinforces the new perception or reverts to old patterns

None of these outcomes are determined by the morphology change. They depend on observer psychology, institutional memory, and social context.

## Part Two: Polyscale Persistence Applied to Clavicular

### The Six Incompatible Timescales in His Intervention

Clavicular is intervening primarily at the **biological timescale** (immediate/months). The polyscale persistence framework predicts that modifications at this timescale will be undermined by persistence at other timescales.

**Timescale 1: Neural (milliseconds)**
- Amygdala response to modified morphology: Likely to shift in direction of "improved" appearance
- **Problem:** Amygdala response is mandatory and pre-conscious. Observers will respond to the new morphology automatically. This timescale does shift with morphology change.
- **Prediction:** Neural timescale effects are favorable to Clavicular

**Timescale 2: Individual (minutes)**
- Observer conscious evaluation of modified appearance: May attribute changes to conscious effort, discipline, or instability
- **Problem:** Whether the morphology change is attributed positively or negatively depends on observer ideology and context
- **Evidence:** In looksmaxxing communities (where Clavicular operates), morphology modification is attributed positively. In mainstream contexts, it may be attributed negatively.
- **Prediction:** Individual timescale effects are context-dependent

**Timescale 3: Developmental (years)**
- Clavicular is age 20. His developmental period is mostly complete.
- **Problem:** The developmental cascade effects (differential treatment accumulating across childhood) have already occurred
- **Prediction:** Clavicular cannot undo developmental cascade effects through current morphological modifications

**Timescale 4: Biographical (decades)**
- Occupational emotional labor demands: Not yet fully determined (Clavicular is 20 years old)
- Expression line development: Will occur at rate determined by occupational demands, not morphology
- Chronic stress accumulation: Will depend on actual social outcomes (employment, relationships), not morphology alone
- **Problem:** If the morphology change does not translate to actual social/occupational opportunity, then stress accumulation will continue at high rates, producing weathering that erodes the surgical benefits
- **Prediction:** If occupational/social outcomes do not improve, the biographical timescale will undo the morphological improvements through accelerated aging

**Timescale 5: Institutional (decades)**
- Institutional memory: Organizations remember how Clavicular was treated previously. A morphology change does not automatically reset institutional memory.
- **Problem:** Clavicular is known in online communities for extreme looksmaxxing and substance use. Institutional memory of this may persist regardless of morphology changes.
- **Evidence:** He has documented his surgeries publicly (livestreamed rhinoplasty). This documentation creates a permanent record of the modification, which observers can access.
- **Prediction:** Institutional persistence will undermine attempts to benefit from the morphology change, because the institutional context remembers the modification itself

**Timescale 6: Algorithmic (milliseconds × millions)**
- Social media algorithms: Will recommend Clavicular's content based on engagement metrics
- **Problem:** The algorithms have likely classified him as a "controversial looksmaxxing content creator." This classification will persist regardless of morphology changes.
- **Prediction:** Algorithmic timescale persistence will continue to affect his online visibility and social position

### The Specific Prediction: Morphological Gains Will Be Undermined

**Scenario 1: Morphology change succeeds (achieves target nasal structure, reduced facial volume, etc.)**

Time 0: Rhinoplasty and related surgeries complete. col(F) has changed to match target morphology.

Time 1 (days to weeks): Neural timescale response. Amygdala responses shift. Observers perceive improved appearance. Initial positive feedback.

Time 2 (weeks to months): Individual timescale response. Some observers attribute the change to discipline/effort (positive), others to vanity/instability (negative). Mixed feedback.

Time 3 (months to years): Institutional and biographical timescale persistence. Clavicular's online documented history becomes relevant. New observers encounter documentation of his extreme looksmaxxing practices (bonesmashing, meth use, multiple surgeries). The morphology change is reinterpreted as evidence of obsession rather than improvement.

Simultaneously, actual occupational/social outcomes depend on factors beyond morphology:
- Communication skills
- Emotional regulation
- Relationship capability
- Occupational opportunity access

If morphology change does not translate to actual improvements in these areas, then stress continues, weathering accelerates, and the morphological gains deteriorate through the aging timescale.

**Thought Experiment 1: The Snowball Reversal**

Clavicular achieves his surgical goals. His nasal structure is reconstructed to match his ideal (narrower, more prominent). His facial volume is reduced through fat loss. His expression lines are minimal (partly due to meth-induced damage to skin, partly due to planned procedures).

For 3 months, he receives positive feedback on his appearance (Timescale 1-2 effects).

But then the feedback stops increasing. This is because:
1. Neural effect has plateaued (observers have adapted to the new appearance)
2. Individual-level positive attribution has reached its limit in his social circles
3. Institutional memory (his extreme practices, documented history) begins to outweigh the morphological improvements
4. Actual social/occupational outcomes have not improved (because morphology was not the limiting factor)
5. Without improvements in actual outcomes, stress continues, and weathering begins to reverse the gains

By year 1 post-surgery, the morphological benefits are diminishing because the biographical/institutional timescales are not supporting them.

### Thought Experiment 2: The Misdirected Investment

Imagine Clavicular had invested the same effort in alternative interventions:

**Scenario A (Actual):** Rhinoplasty, wisdom teeth extraction, otoplasty, multiple surgeries, bonesmashing, crystal meth for fat loss, multiple experimental pharmaceuticals, planned bimaxillary osteotomy

Cost: ~$200,000 (surgeries) + unknown long-term health costs + lost time to recovery
Outcome at 5 years: Possibly some persistent morphological changes, but underlying X₁ (ancestry) remains unchanged, X₆ (character) unaffected

**Scenario B (Hypothetical Alternative):** Invest equivalent time/money in:
- Therapy/coaching for social skills, communication
- Occupational training for high-status employment
- Relational/dating skills coaching
- Stress reduction practices

Cost: ~$50,000 (coaching, therapy)
Outcome at 5 years: Actual improvements in social/occupational outcomes, which reduce X₄ (chronic stress), which reduce weathering, which improve actual biological health markers, which improve observable morphology through health signals (better skin, fuller faces, etc.)

**The comparison:** Scenario B would likely produce better long-term outcomes (better health, better actual social results, less chronic stress) at lower cost with lower risk. But it requires changing X₆ (behavior/capability/social skills) rather than changing col(F) (observable morphology).

Clavicular has chosen the col/ker error: investing entirely in changing col(F) while assuming it will change ker(F).

- Whether observers interpret the changes as "improved" or "obsessive"

**Prediction:** If bimaxillary osteotomy is performed without addressing underlying social/occupational factors, it will produce similar long-term results to rhinoplasty: initial positive response, followed by institutional/biographical timescale persistence that undermines the gains.

## Part Four: The Institutional Context - Looksmaxxing Movement

### What Clavicular Is Actually Operating Within

The looksmaxxing movement is a subculture organized around the belief that physical appearance (primarily facial morphology) is the dominant determinant of social value, particularly dating value.

**Core ideology:**
- Facial morphology determines trustworthiness, dominance, attractiveness
- Modifying facial morphology through surgery and substances can improve social outcomes
- This improvement is a rational investment in future quality of life

**The col/ker error embedded in the ideology:**
The looksmaxxing movement operates entirely under the col/ker error. It treats observable facial morphology (col(F)) as if it is the primary determinant of actual character, capability, and social value (ker(F)).

**Empirical facts that challenge the ideology:**
- Facial morphology explains 2-5% of variance in dating success
- Height explains ~10-15%
- Income explains ~20-30%
- Social skills, emotional regulation, occupational status, and cultural capital explain much more

- Facial morphology explains <5% of variance in job performance or career success
- The hiring discrimination based on appearance is real, but the effect size is much smaller than looksmaxxers believe
- Once someone is hired, morphology has essentially zero correlation with actual job performance

**Why the ideology is appealing:**
1. It attributes social failure to a specific, measurable problem (facial morphology) rather than to complex social/occupational/relational factors
2. It offers a concrete solution (surgery, substances) rather than requiring abstract behavioral change
3. It creates community (looksmaxxing movement) for people who feel socially marginalized
4. It provides narrative coherence ("If I just fix my face, everything will change")

**Why the ideology is problematic:**
1. It misdirects effort toward interventions with low benefit-to-cost ratio
2. It creates escalating commitment (when the first surgery doesn't produce expected results, the response is "more surgery," not "reassess the theory")
3. It normalizes extreme health-damaging behaviors (crystal meth, bonesmashing, experimental drug use)
4. It replicates the col/ker error at community scale: the movement as a whole operates under a fundamental category mistake

### Clavicular's Role in Propagating the Error

**What he is doing:**
- Documenting extreme looksmaxxing practices
- Livestreaming surgeries
- Advocating for risky interventions
- Creating content that attracts followers interested in extreme modification

**The effect he is creating:**

**The empirical outcome:**
- The livestreamed rhinoplasty documentation is visible to anyone
- The subsequent recovery, results, and actual social outcomes are also observable
- This creates a real-world experiment in whether morphological modification produces the expected social outcomes

**Prediction:** Over the next 12-36 months, as Clavicular's post-surgical outcomes become clearer (how much actual dating/social/occupational improvement he experiences, whether the gains persist, whether the health costs accrue), the documentation will serve as empirical evidence for or against the looksmaxxing ideology.

If the morphological improvements fail to produce corresponding social improvements, this will be visible to his followers. This is data.

## Part Five: Thought Experiments Illustrating the Core Problem

### Thought Experiment 3: The Morphology-Only Counterfactual

Consider two individuals, identical in all respects except facial morphology:

**Individual A:**
- Actual capability: High (intelligent, emotionally regulated, socially skilled, hardworking)
- Actual character: Trustworthy, honest, competent
- Facial morphology: Unattractive by mainstream standards
- Looksmaxxing history: No surgeries or modifications

**Individual B:**
- Actual capability: High (identical to A)
- Actual character: Trustworthy, honest, competent (identical to A)
- Facial morphology: Attractive by mainstream standards
- Looksmaxxing history: No surgeries or modifications

**Prediction (from col/ker framework):**
- Short term (neural/individual timescale): B receives better first impressions and higher trustworthiness ratings
- Medium term (occupational timescale): B is hired more frequently for appearance-sensitive roles (customer-facing, etc.)
- Long term (biographical timescale): If both perform equally at jobs (because actual capability is identical), then repeated positive performance feedback for both should eventually overcome initial morphological differences
- Very long term (institutional timescale): Both should accumulate similar career outcomes if occupational performance is equal

**The empirical data:** Studies on physical attractiveness effects show:
- Initial hiring advantage for attractive individuals: 15-25% higher callback rates for identical resumes
- On-the-job performance: No correlation between attractiveness and actual job performance
- Career outcomes: Initial attractiveness advantage partially persists through career, but is mediated by initial hiring differences and confidence differences (attractive people are treated better, so develop higher confidence)

**The implication for Clavicular:** Even if he achieves attractiveness parity with highly attractive individuals, his actual occupational outcomes still depend on:
1. Whether he can convert the initial impression advantage into actual job performance
2. Whether he can maintain the advantage through actual performance over time
3. Whether the institutional systems support his continued advancement

If his actual capability (X₆ character/competence) is not high enough to sustain the advantages created by improved morphology, then the advantages will erode.

### Thought Experiment 4: The Escalation Trap

**Baseline scenario:** Clavicular gets rhinoplasty. The surgery succeeds. His nasal structure is reconstructed to his ideal specification.

**Initial outcome:** Positive feedback. People comment on his appearance. He receives more matches on dating apps. More people are interested in his content.

**But then:** The feedback plateaus. People adapt to his new appearance. The initial novelty wears off.

**Clavicular's options:**

**Option A:** Conclude that the strategy is working and plan additional surgeries (bimaxillary osteotomy, limb-lengthening, etc.)

- Effect: Escalating commitment to morphological modification
- Outcome: More surgery, more recovery time, more health risk
- Prediction: Each surgery produces diminishing returns (first surgery 25% satisfaction increase, second surgery 10% increase, third surgery 5% increase)

**Option B:** Conclude that the morphology was never the primary limiting factor and reassess the theory

- Effect: Might shift focus to behavioral/occupational factors that actually predict outcomes
- Outcome: Different investment strategy (therapy, coaching, occupational development)
- Prediction: Better long-term outcomes

**The trap:** Once committed to the col/ker error ideology (especially publicly, through livestreamed surgeries), it becomes very difficult to change course. The documented history creates institutional lock-in.

If Clavicular were to conclude "Actually, facial morphology doesn't matter as much as I thought, and I should focus on other factors," he would be admitting that:
1. The previous surgeries were misdirected effort
2. The documented history is not evidence of effective strategy
3. His influence on followers is based on a false theory

This is psychologically difficult. The typical response is escalation: "I haven't done enough modification yet. The next surgery will work."

### Thought Experiment 5: The Health-Outcome Trade-off

**Clavicular's current intervention portfolio:**

**Net comparison:**

**Outcome prediction at 10 years:**
- Clavicular's strategy: Morphological changes persist, health costs accrue (neurological damage from meth, complications from surgeries)
